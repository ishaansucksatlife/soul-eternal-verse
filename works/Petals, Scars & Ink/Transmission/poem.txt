The phone itself.

It’s just metal, glass, and lithium.

It doesn't know who she is.
It doesn't care.

It weighs only a few ounces
in your palm.

Nothing about it suggests significance.

Nothing about it suggests
that entire evenings
can become trapped inside it.

ㅤ
The room remains dark.

The house remains quiet.

Hallways disappear into shadow.
Doors stand closed.

The world beyond the walls
surrenders itself to sleep.

ㅤ
Then the screen lights.

A pale blue rectangle
against the darkness.

Sharp. Artificial.

The only thing in the room
willing to announce its existence.

Its glow reaches the walls,
the desk,
the edge of the bed,

turning familiar objects
into silhouettes.

ㅤ
And somewhere beyond that light,

she exists.

Hundreds of miles away.

Entire cities away.

Entire nights away.

ㅤ
The screen cannot reduce that distance.

Only illuminate it.

ㅤ
Then the dots appear.

Three small circles.

Rising.
Falling.
Returning.

ㅤ
Nothing more.

Yet suddenly
the room feels occupied.

ㅤ
Those dots are movement.

Those dots are thought.

A sentence still becoming itself.

An idea crossing the distance
before language arrives to carry it.

ㅤ
For a few seconds,

she exists as anticipation.

Not words.

Not sound.

Only the visible evidence
that somewhere,

right now,

her attention is pointed
in the same direction.

ㅤ
The dots vanish.

The screen stills.

The room exhales.

Then her message arrives.

ㅤ
A line of text.

A voice note.

A photograph.

Tiny arrangements of data.

Electrical signals
translated into meaning.

ㅤ
The phone acts as a conduit.

A tether.

A boundary.

A machine built entirely
for the transportation of absence.

ㅤ
Your hand touches glass.

Your mind touches her.

ㅤ
The voice enters through a speaker.

Compressed.
Encoded.
Transmitted.
Decoded.

Yet somehow
the warmth survives.

ㅤ
Maybe she laughs.

Maybe her voice lowers slightly
because it is late where she is.

Maybe there is tiredness in it.

Maybe comfort.

Maybe something neither language
nor technology
knows how to name.

ㅤ
The sound travels effortlessly.

Across towers.

Across wires.

Across satellites.

Across miles.

ㅤ
Then stops completely

at the surface of the screen.

ㅤ
The device can carry her voice.

Not her presence.

It can reproduce every word.

Not the space she occupies.

ㅤ
And still,

a fullness forms.

Constructed entirely from light.

Entirely from sound.

Entirely from a surface

that has no texture at all.

ㅤ
Time passes unnoticed.

Messages become conversations.

Conversations become hours.

ㅤ
The room remains dark.

The house remains silent.

Yet neither feels empty.

Not while she is there.

ㅤ
Eventually,

the screen is locked.

ㅤ
The blue light disappears.

The room returns all at once.

Walls.

Shadows.

Silence.

ㅤ
The display turns black,

reflecting a face back
from the dark.

A faint outline.

A quiet observer.

ㅤ
The phone rests in your hand.

Slightly warm now.

Battery heat.

Electrical heat.

Mechanical heat.

ㅤ
The only warmth left in the room.

Not hers.

ㅤ
The conversation is over.

The notification is gone.

The current has stopped flowing.

ㅤ
Yet something has shifted.

ㅤ
Her voice was here moments ago.

Her laughter occupied the air.

Her thoughts crossed the distance.

The room made space for them.

ㅤ
Now it must learn

how to exist without them again.

ㅤ
The house settles.

The darkness returns.

The screen remains black.

ㅤ
And somewhere beyond the walls,

beyond the city,

beyond the miles,

she continues existing

outside the reach

of glass,

of lithium,

of light.

ㅤ
Until the screen wakes again.
