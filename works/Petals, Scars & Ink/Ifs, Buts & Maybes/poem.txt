
‎‎‎‎‎‎The cruelest things
rarely leave cleanly.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
They remain in fragments,
in unfinished sentences,
in chairs still angled toward conversation,
in habits that continue existing
long after the reason for them
has gone cold.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And afterward,
the mind begins its slow infection.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
If.
But.
Maybe.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Words so small
they almost escape notice.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Yet they hollow people out
from the inside.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
An if appears first.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Quietly.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
If something had been said differently.
If the silence had been interrupted sooner.
If certain nights had ended
before becoming memories.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
If less distance
had entered the room unnoticed.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
If softness
had not been mistaken for permanence.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The mind circles these things endlessly,
touching old conversations
like bruises that never settled correctly.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Because absence
does not leave quietly.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
It loosens meaning from memories,
turns certainty into speculation,
until even the clearest moments
begin feeling rewritten
by distance.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Then come the buts.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
But they once cared.
But there were signs.
But people do not disappear
without hesitation.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
A but is grief
trying to negotiate with reality.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
A slow rearranging of truth
into shapes gentle enough
to survive beside.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The unbearable thing
is how believable it sounds.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
How easily the heart accepts
small alterations to memory
if it means preserving
even the faintest illusion
that something real remained underneath.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And then,
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
the maybes.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The longest deaths
are always hidden inside maybes.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Maybe the leaving
was not intentional.
Maybe distance only looked like indifference.
Maybe silence meant confusion,
not absence.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Maybe.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Maybe.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Maybe.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The word repeats
until meaning dissolves from it completely.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Still, it continues breathing.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
That is the problem.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
A maybe never decays properly.
It stays preserved
like flowers trapped between pages,
dead long ago
but still carrying shape.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And eventually
they begin merging together.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The ifs feeding the buts.
The buts protecting the maybes.
The maybes resurrecting the ifs again.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
An endless machinery of grief.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
If things had been different,
but perhaps they couldn’t have been.
Maybe the ending
had already begun
long before it became visible.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
If the signs were real,
but memory distorts everything it touches.
Maybe the warmth
was only temporary
from the beginning.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And somewhere inside it all,
the mind keeps building corridors
that lead nowhere.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Returning to the same moments,
the same voices,
the same unfinished exits,
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
trying desperately
to locate a version of events
that hurts less.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
It never does.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
There are people
who never truly leave.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Not because they stayed,
but because departure
was never explained enough
to become final.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
So they linger.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Inside songs avoided halfway through.
Inside certain streets at night.
Inside the unbearable quiet
after laughter ends too suddenly.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Their absence develops routines.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
It sits beside exhaustion.
Sleeps beside memory.
Learns the architecture
of ordinary days.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And over time,
what hurts stops being the leaving itself.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
It becomes the reconstruction.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The endless rebuilding
of moments that no longer defend themselves.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Every glance examined again.
Every silence reopened.
Every ending disassembled carefully
like a crime scene
hoping to uncover
something survivable beneath it.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Usually, there isn’t.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Because some people leave twice.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Once in body.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And again later,
slowly,
through realization.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Through the understanding
that certain kindnesses
were temporary shelters,
not homes.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
That some promises
only existed
while convenient to keep.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The worst part
is not abandonment.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
It is how ordinary everything looked
before it happened.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
How laughter continued normally.
How voices remained warm.
How nothing announced itself
before becoming irreversible.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
So the mind keeps returning.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
To the ifs.
The buts.
The maybes.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Tiny words
dragging enormous graves behind them.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And somewhere beneath all of it
sits the quietest thought of all,
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
that perhaps
those who left
had already been leaving
for a very long time.
