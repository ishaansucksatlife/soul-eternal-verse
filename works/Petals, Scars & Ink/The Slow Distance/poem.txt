No one tells you
that placing someone
at the centre of your sky
means the eclipse will be yours to survive.
ㅤ
You shift your gravity toward them.
Rearrange your hours.
Edit your sentences mid-thought
so they land softer in their hands.
You make room in your day
the way people make room for something sacred,
carefully, reverently,
without realizing what you’re giving up.
ㅤ
It never begins loudly.
It begins with seconds.
A reply that used to arrive instantly
now sitting unread.
A laugh that once lingered
now trimmed at the edges.
A warmth that cools
just enough
to make you question
whether it was ever warmth at all.
ㅤ
You tell yourself you’re overthinking.
That silence is harmless.
That distance is temporary.
That people don’t wake up one day
and feel less.
ㅤ
But they do not wake up.
They fade.
ㅤ
And fading is quieter
than leaving.
ㅤ
You keep offering weight
to their words,
to their moods,
to their presence,
as if your attention could anchor them.
Meanwhile, you become lighter in their world.
Easier to postpone.
Easier to forget.
ㅤ
They are still there.
Just altered.
Like a home after a fire
where the walls remain standing
but everything inside
has turned to ash.
ㅤ
There is a gap now.
Small enough to pretend doesn’t exist.
Wide enough to swallow sleep.
It lives between “I’m tired”
and the conversations you no longer have.
It hums in the space
where effort used to be mutual.
ㅤ
You measure it in minutes.
You feel it in lifetimes.
ㅤ
You miss the way they once leaned toward you
without calculation.
The way you never had to earn
what was freely given.
The way existing
didn’t feel like negotiating.
ㅤ
Now every interaction feels balanced on a scale,
how much you say,
how much you give,
how much you hold back
so you don’t appear desperate
for something that used to be natural.
ㅤ
You want the old air back.
The old ease.
The version of them
that did not feel like it was
slowly stepping away
while still close enough
to touch.
ㅤ
But some shifts are permanent.
Some silences are decisions.
Some distances are chosen
long before they are spoken.
ㅤ
And the cruelest truth is not that they left.
ㅤ
It’s that you stayed,
watching them withdraw inch by inch,
convincing yourself
that if you just held on tighter,
if you just gave a little more,
if you just became smaller,
quieter,
easier,
ㅤ
they might return
to the way they once were.
ㅤ
But they don’t.
ㅤ
They don’t come back to the version
you’re still grieving
while they stand in front of you.
ㅤ
And one day you realize
the space between you
is no longer a gap.
ㅤ
It is a grave
for something that did not die all at once,
ㅤ
but slowly,
mercilessly,
while you were still calling it yours.
