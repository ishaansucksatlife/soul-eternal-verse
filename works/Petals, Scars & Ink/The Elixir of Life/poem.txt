The day begins
with something dark enough
to resemble understanding.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Poured slowly,
it settles into the cup
without resistance,
black, quiet, familiar.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Steam rises from it
like a thought
too fragile to keep.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Hands reach instinctively,
not for taste,
but for temperature.
For proof
that something can still be held
without pulling away.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Coffee is never sweet on its own.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
People interfere with it.
Milk. Sugar.
Small white lies
folded into the bitterness
to make it seem
less severe than it is.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
They watch the darkness pale,
watch the spoon circle,
watch sweetness disappear into it
as though softening the color
could soften the thing itself.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The surface changes.
The truth doesn’t.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Underneath,
it remains exactly
what it was poured as.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
There is something intimate
about drinking heat
when everything else feels distant.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Something humiliating
about needing a cup
to fill the silence
left by things
that once had names.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The first sip always burns.
Not enough to stop.
Just enough to register.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
A controlled injury.
A chosen one.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
After that,
the mouth adjusts.
It learns what to ignore.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
People do too.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
This is why the second sip
goes down easier.
Not because it hurts less,
but because the body
has already begun
making room for it.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
At the bottom of the cup
there is always residue,
thin brown lines,
a dark settling,
evidence that some things
never fully dissolve
no matter how long
they are stirred.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Still, it is consumed.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Morning after morning,
the same ritual of swallowing
what was never pleasant,
only familiar.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Some drinks are taken for thirst.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
This one is taken
for the space between moments,
for the stillness at tables
with only one set of hands,
for windows looked through
without seeing.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
It cools if left untouched.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
A slow abandonment.
The surface dims.
The warmth retreats inward.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Then it becomes harder to drink,
not because it changed,
but because it stopped pretending
to be comforting.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Cold coffee tastes closer
to what it always meant.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Flat.
Bitter.
Lingering.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
There are cups
finished absentmindedly,
and there are cups
held long after empty,
fingers around ceramic
as if delay itself
could postpone returning
to whatever waits outside it.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
The stain remains on the rim.
A faint outline of contact.
Proof that something touched you
and left.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
By evening,
another cup is made.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Darker this time.
Less sugar.
Less effort.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
No one notices
how often bitterness
becomes a habit.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
No one notices
how easily warmth
gets mistaken for company.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
Coffee keeps arriving
in the same quiet form,
dark enough to hide in,
hot enough to hurt,
temporary enough
to disappear.
‎‎‎‎‎‎‎‎ㅤ
And each day
it is lifted to the mouth
like a rehearsed acceptance
of things that do not get better,
only easier
to swallow.
