They didn’t come as warnings.
They came as routine,
as people who already knew
where you softened,
where you didn’t look twice.

ㅤ
They spoke in your tone,
borrowed your ease,
sat close enough
to be mistaken for safety.

ㅤ
You never question
what feels practiced.
You never guard yourself
against what feels like home.

ㅤ
They learn you slowly,
not with care,
but with precision.
Every habit, every hesitation,
mapped without you noticing.

ㅤ
Not to understand you.
To handle you.

ㅤ
There’s a kind of betrayal
that doesn’t break doors.
It rearranges the room
while you’re still inside it.

ㅤ
Nothing obvious,
just small misplacements.
A word shifted here.
A meaning tilted there.

ㅤ
And suddenly
you’re being discussed
as a version of yourself
you never agreed to become.

ㅤ
They don’t lie outright.
That would be easier to catch.

ㅤ
They edit.
They trim.
They present you
with just enough truth
to make the damage believable.

ㅤ
You laugh together,
but something always feels… delayed.
Like they’re half a step ahead
in a conversation
you thought you were leading.

ㅤ
You don’t hear it directly.
You feel it in absence,
in the way rooms change temperature
when you enter,
in how sentences stop
a second too soon.

ㅤ
Your name travels
without your consent,
gathering weight
from mouths you trusted.

ㅤ
And the strange part is,
they never leave.

ㅤ
They stay close.
Closer than most.
Offering advice,
offering presence,
offering a version of loyalty
that looks complete
until you look at it twice.

ㅤ
Because this isn’t hatred.
Hatred would have distance.
Would have clarity.

ㅤ
This is something quieter,
something that survives
by being mistaken for care.

ㅤ
You start to notice it
in reflections of yourself
you don’t recognize,
in opinions attributed to you
you never formed.

ㅤ
And when you trace it back,
it leads not outward,
but inward,
to the same circle
you thought would hold.

ㅤ
That’s the fracture.

ㅤ
Not that they turned,
but that they never stood
where you believed they did.

ㅤ
And even then,
they’ll stand beside you,
unchanged,
unmoved,
speaking to you
with the same voice
that spoke about you,

ㅤ
as if nothing was ever divided.
